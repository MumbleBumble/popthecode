Why?

Suppose you're at dinner with a friend. Midway through dinner, you begin to argue over a certain site you've both visited.

"Come on!", he insists, "you know as well as I do that that entire site was done in bootstrap! Just look at the navbar!"

Unperturbed, you decide to prove him wrong (the site was clearly made with normalize). You reach into your pocket and pull out your iPhone...

Only to realize you don't feel like mucking about with irritating "bookmarklets" (whatever those are) or bending over backwards to buy (BUY!) an app just so you can see some page source. How un-free (as in free speech) is that?

Fortunately, you don't have to. With PopTheCode.com you can simply pop the hood and take a look at what's happening behind the scenes. Even if you're at your computer, you can use PopTheCode.com to see what a site would look like if you were viewing it from another browser, or another device

So jump to it!

How?

By the magic of Python. Really, it's not that complicated.

Forgoing both bootstrap and normalize (shudder), PopTheCode.com was sloppily coded and styled by hand.
